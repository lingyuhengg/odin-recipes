Instant Pot Birria

This is a recipe for Birria made using an Instant Pot. For a more precise recipe, click here.

Ingredients

  • 1kg Chuck roast beef
  • 3 Dried ancho chiles
  • 3 Dried guajillo chilies
  • 1 Dried chile de arbol
  • 1 Onion, sliced
  • 6 Garlic cloves, sliced
  • Unsalted stock (chicken, beef, etc.)
  • Tomato Paste, 2 tablespoons
  • Other seasonings to liking (cinnamon, cumin, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Dried Chilies

    Remove the stems and seeds from the dried chilies. The chilies can be toasted but not necessary for making Birria with the Instant Pot.

  2. Brown Meat in Instant Pot

    Set the Instant Pot to 'Saute' and wait for it to come to temperature. While waiting, season the beef with salt and pepper. When the pot comes to temperature, add 1 tablespoon of oil into the pot and sear all sides of the beef until it turns brown. Set aside the browned meat.

  3. Saute Onions and Garlic

    Add in another tablespoon of oil, before adding the sliced onions and chopped garlic into the pot and sauteing for until fragrant. After that, add the tomato paste as well as any other seasoning (bay leaves, mexican oregano, cinnamon, etc.) and saute until fragrant as well.

  4. Deglaze Instant Pot

    Pour in 1 cup of stock, and deglaze the pot by using a wooden spoon to scrap the bottom of the pot while mixing the stock around.

  5. Pressure Cook Birria

    Add the dried chiles as well as another cup of stock, and give the pot a mix. Layer the browned beef on top as well. Seal the Instant Pot and turn the Venting Knob to the sealing position. Pressure Cook at High Pressure for 40 minutes, followed by Natural Release for 15 minutes.

  6. Shred Beef and Blend Sauce

    Transfer the beef to a large mixing bowl and shred it to the desired size.

    Remove any unconsumable foods (cinnamon sticks, bay leaves) from the pot and blend the remaining sauce with either an immersion blender or a food processor. Once blended, add the shredded beef back into the sauce. Season the birria to taste as necessary.

  7. Serve Birria

    Prepare some birria with other staples, such asa tacos and cheese. The birria can also be garnished with cilantro and diced onions as well.
